# OnZone App Documentation

OnZone is a productivity app designed to help Egyptian students focus, manage distractions, and build effective study habits with meaningful rewards.

**Phase 1 – Empathize & Define (Aug 19 – Sep 5)**: Research productivity apps, survey Egyptian students on pain points, identify core issues.

**Phase 2 – Ideate (Sep 6 – Sep 20)**: Brainstorm and prioritize features, create initial sketches, and low-fidelity wireframes.

**Phase 3 – Prototype (Sep 21 – Oct 15)**: Develop mid- and high-fidelity mockups on Figma. Incorporate Arabic, local payment workflows, and accessible design.

**Phase 4 – Test & Iterate (Oct 16 – Nov 10)**: Conduct usability tests and iteratively refine flows, focusing on accessibility and feedback.

**Phase 5 – Finalize & Present (Nov 11 – Nov 15)**: Deliver final design deck and prototype. Document research, personas, and the complete design process.

## Requirement Gathering

### Functional Requirements
- **Focus Zone**: Customizable timer to block distractions and suggest tasks.
- **To-Do List**: Create and manage tasks by priority, deadline, and category.
- **Study Zone**: File uploads and study materials support.
- **Reminder System**: Calendar and alarms to notify about incomplete tasks.

### User Research Insights
- **Challenges**: Students struggle with notifications, social media, and boredom. 100% of survey respondents reported distractions causing study disruptions.
- **Needs**: A central, AI-assisted app to improve concentration and study organization.

### Key Non-Functional Requirements
- **Simplicity**: Two-step access to core features.
- **Accessibility**: Adjustable UI for inclusivity.
- **Performance**: Lightweight app for optimized speed.
- **Localization**: Arabic-enabled interface and local payment flows.

### Exclusions at MVP Stage
- No YouTube API for streaming.
- No AI enhancements or payment integrations.
- No group study or collaborative tools.

## Problem Statement
Egyptian students face constant distractions like social media and noisy environments, lacking effective tools tailored to their needs. OnZone addresses these challenges by offering a centralized solution to manage distractions and enable focused study habits.

## Project Objectives
Enable distraction-free study through easy focus sessions, task management, and motivation. Ultimately, the goal is for OnZone to be the go-to app for student productivity in Egypt.

### Value Proposition
OnZone is designed for Egyptian student realities like noisy homes, tight schedules, and digital distractions. Every feature addresses the most relevant pain points.
